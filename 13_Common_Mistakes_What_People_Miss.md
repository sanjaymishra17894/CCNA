# 30 Common Mistakes in CCNA - What People Miss

1. **Not Understanding OSI Model**  
   Many candidates skip over the OSI model, which is critical for troubleshooting and understanding how data travels across networks.

   ### Real-World Example:  
   When faced with connectivity issues, a solid understanding of the OSI layers helps pinpoint where the problem lies.

   ### Lab Practice:  
   Set up a simple network and simulate problems at different OSI layers.

2. **Ignoring Subnetting**  
   Subnetting is often a point of confusion that can lead to IP address conflicts and wasted addresses.

   ### Real-World Example:  
   Misconfiguring subnets can isolate devices from each other in a network.

   ### Lab Practice:  
   Practice subnetting exercises using different CIDR notations.

3. **Overlooking VLAN Configuration**  
   Not properly configuring VLANs can lead to network segmentation issues.

   ### Real-World Example:  
   Devices on different VLANs may not communicate if the VLANs are not configured on the switch.

   ### Lab Practice:  
   Create multiple VLANs and test communication between them.

4. **Failing to Configure Routing Protocols**  
   Candidates may forget to enable necessary routing protocols, leading to routing issues.

   ### Real-World Example:  
   Without routing protocols like OSPF or EIGRP, routers won’t know how to forward packets.

   ### Lab Practice:  
   Set up a lab with multiple routers and configure OSPF.

5. **Not Understanding Troubleshooting Commands**  
   Many overlook critical commands like `ping`, `traceroute`, and `show` commands, which are vital for diagnosing issues.

   ### Real-World Example:  
   Failing to use `ping` to test connectivity can lead to missed simple issues.

   ### Lab Practice:  
   Use troubleshooting commands in various scenarios to diagnose problems.

6. **Assuming All Hardware is Compatible**  
   Not checking hardware compatibility can lead to failures and wasted time.

   ### Real-World Example:  
   Mixing different vendor devices can cause interoperability issues.

   ### Lab Practice:  
   Research hardware compatibility lists before setting up a network.

7. **Misconfiguring Access Lists**  
   Access control lists (ACLs) can either block or allow traffic; misconfigurations can lead to significant issues.

   ### Real-World Example:  
   A wrongly placed ACL could block essential traffic, disrupting services.

   ### Lab Practice:  
   Create different ACLs and test their effects on network traffic.

8. **Not Using Network Documentation**  
   Without proper documentation, troubleshooting becomes exponentially harder.

   ### Real-World Example:  
   A lack of documentation can lead to configuration drift over time.

   ### Lab Practice:  
   Document a lab network and refer to the documentation during troubleshooting.

9. **Underestimating the Importance of Security**  
   Security practices like disabling unused ports and using strong passwords are often overlooked.

   ### Real-World Example:  
   Unsecured ports can lead to network breaches.

   ### Lab Practice:  
   Implement security best practices on your lab setups.

10. **Not Testing Failover Mechanisms**  
   Candidates sometimes configure redundancies but fail to test them.

   ### Real-World Example:  
   In a disaster recovery situation, untested failovers can lead to prolonged outages.

   ### Lab Practice:  
   Simulate failover scenarios and test your configurations.
