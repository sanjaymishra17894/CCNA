# 3-Month Study Plan

## Month 1
- Review basic networking concepts.
- Familiarize yourself with the exam syllabus and objectives.
- Start with foundational topics like OSI model, TCP/IP, subnetting, etc.

## Month 2
- Dive into routing protocols (RIP, OSPF, EIGRP).
- Practice configuration and troubleshooting.
- Use labs to strengthen hands-on skills.

## Month 3
- Focus on security concepts and technologies.
- Take full-length practice exams.
- Schedule your exam date and make a final review.


# 6-Month Study Plan

## Month 1-2
- In-depth review of all core networking concepts.
- Watch video lectures and read textbooks.

## Month 3-4
- Hands-on labs.
- Focus on advanced routing and switching topics.

## Month 5
- Get comfortable with multiple practice exams.
- Review weak areas and study accordingly.

## Month 6
- Finalize exam preparation with a review of all topics.
- Pass guarantee tips: Ensure you study consistently and seek help where needed.


# Exam Preparation Strategy
- Create a study schedule and stick to it.
- Join study groups for sharing knowledge and resources.
- Utilize online resources and forums for clarification on tough topics.


# Time Management Tips
- Allocate specific time slots for studying each topic.
- Avoid procrastination by setting clear goals.
- Use techniques like Pomodoro for effective study sessions.


# Mock Test Recommendations
- Use reliable resources like Boson or MeasureUp for practice tests.
- Aim for a minimum of 5-10 practice exams before the actual test.


# Pass Guarantee Tips
- Focus on understanding concepts rather than rote memorization.
- Regularly revise topics you find challenging.
